**Key Facts**
- Origins date to 1950s, popularized as faxlore in 1980s
- Institutions named have denied link
- Typical claim: lose 10 pounds in a week
- National Enquirer popularized recipe 1995-1996
- Provides <1,000 cal days 1-3, 1,000-1,200 days 4-7
- No scientific evidence for safety/effectiveness
- Only ~34% of first-week loss from fat
- Side effects include dehydration, headaches, dizziness
- Increases gallstone risk
- Ranked most absurd diet in UK study
